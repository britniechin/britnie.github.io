HCDE 517 Client Based Project: Tableau Desktop

I had the opproutunity as an undergraduate to sit in a graduate level class, with permission from the instructor. For this class HCDE 517: Usability Studies we got an opprotunity to work in groups on a client based project. My groups was given the opprotunity to work with Tableau with the Desktop team. In about 10 weeks we conducted a usability study and presented our findings to the class and the Tableau team.

Purpose & Goal

The purpose of this study was to learn more about how beginner Tableau Desktop users experience the dashboard creation and interaction process. Specifically, our goal was to discover pain points and barriers beginners face when using Tableau to create dashboards as well as provide recommendations for improvements and next steps. In doing so, we hoped to provide useful, data driven insights into how the dashboard experience can be improved for beginners thereby making it more accessible to them.

Partcipant Overview & Scope

We had a total of seven partcipants for this study, with 4 male and 3 female partciapnts averaging at the age of 28. We looked for partciapnts that created 1-10 dashboard and that have used Tableau within the last four months. We wanted partcipants that were considered intermediate users and looked at how they used Tableau in the past. We considered partciapnts who played with the dashboard for personal projects to occasionally creating simple dashboards for work. We also noticed 5 out of the 7 partcipants worked in the tech field and had experience used Excel or R.

Methods & Procedures

Our team conducted 7 moderated, in person usability test sessions at the University of Washington Odegaard undergraduate Library and Sieg Hall. Prior to eahc session we asked every partciapnt a series of pre-test questions guaging things like their current attitudes towards Tableau and their use patterns. We presented them with a scenario which proivded context for the 20 tasks they were given. Upon completing all tasks, each partciapnt were asked a series of post-test questions to reflect on their experiences as well as the Net Promoter question. All but one partcipant was administered the System Usability Scale.

Methods we used during this study was moderated usability testing and thinking out loud. We wanted the thinking out loud method and session recordings for each session, to look back on to see facial expressions and understand there thought process as they completed each task. During the study the moderator interacted directly with the participant and guided them through the study by reading the script, answering questions, and probing the partcipant. While the note taker was there to write any notes or observations from the session.

Findings

From this usability study we were able to learn a lot from beginner partcipants and understand the pain points they had and the things they liked about Tableau. We were able to analyze the data by reviewing the session recordings and affinity mapping with sticky notes. The affinity diagram was successful in terms of organizing each partciapnt with a different color sticky notes and noticing repetive patterns within the partcipants. From our data we noticed:

  • The Hide & Unhide Features were confusing
  • The "Show Dashboard Title" Function was difficult to find
  • There were inconsistencies in sheet and visualization titles
  • Partcipants got stuck in the map task and couldn't get back
  • Partcipants had trouble filtering visualization and retrieving filter if it was removed

Recommendations

From our findings we recommend to combine the name of the sheet/dashboard with the tab titles for more consistency. As well as the "Show Dashboard Title" to be more salient and to have heirachy within the UI, in the current state the Dashboard Title is at the bottom and hard for partcipants to notice. We also recommend to redesign components to be more obvious, because Tableau is such a powerful tool and there's a lot of commands and things you can do we recommend to make components more obvious to see and notice.

Takeways

If I had the opportunity to run this study again I'd specifically reach out to non-students and students, orginally we only recruited partcipants that went to the University of Washington which made recruiting and the scope small. Since we mainly focused on finding UW students we tried slack groups, messaging different departments, and trying to find students who've used Tableau. Another thing I'd change is be more clear that partcipants won't be judged on their skills using Tableau, we had several potential partcipants shy away from partcipant because they felt Tableau was daunting and didn't want to be judged on performance or failing tasks. I'd also change and have more practice sessions to check for and resolve any techinal issues for running the study. Lastly, I'd want to give the option for partcipants to have a PC option, because some partcipants were not familair with Macs and the layout of Tableau slightly changing.

What I learned

From this experience I learned a lot from the project itself, but also working with graduate level students. I learned how to work with a client and design a usability study end-to-end and how to collaboratively and effectively run testing as a team. I also learned how to use Tableau, although I wished I learned or was more familiar because partcipants stumbled and got away from the goal and I couldn't help them find there way back. I also learned how to draw meaningful conclusions from a usability study data, and how to run and design a usability test on a complex product unfamiliar to me.
